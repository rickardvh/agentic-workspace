# Recurring Friction Ledger Decision

## Decision

Keep the recurring-friction ledger as a compact runbook for weak, repeated package friction. Move durable rationale and trade-off context here so the runbook stays procedural.

## Rationale

Recurring friction often starts below the threshold for an issue or active plan. Preserving a short recurrence trail prevents the same weak signal from resetting across sessions, but the runbook should not become a backlog, incident history, or product doctrine file.

The ledger exists because promotion should be evidence-backed rather than hindsight-driven. Once the same friction class clearly repeats, the stronger home should be planning, docs, tests, validation, automation, or code.

## Boundaries

- The runbook owns capture mechanics and the entry format.
- This decision note owns why the ledger exists and why it should remain compact.
- Issue bodies, execution logs, and narrative history belong elsewhere.
- If entries accumulate without promotion, treat that as a signal to remove, automate, or route the friction.

## Review Trigger

Review this decision when the ledger stops shrinking after promotion, starts duplicating issue bodies, or becomes a first-line surface for ordinary startup.
